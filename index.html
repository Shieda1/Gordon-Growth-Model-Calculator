What is the gordon growth model?
the gordon growth model is a calculation of a firm's intrinsic value assuming that it's shares are worth the sum of it's future dividends discounted back to their present value.
How to calculate gordon growth model?
To calculate gordon growth model you should use the following formula:
p = D1 / (r - g)
where:
p = current stock price
g = constant growth rate expected for dividends,in perpetuity
r = constant cost of equity capital for the company(rate of return)
D1 = value of next year's dividends
example: A company's stock is trading at $160 per share. The company requires 6% minimum rate of return (r) and it will pay a $2 dividends per share in the next year(D1) which is expected to increase by 4% annually (g), calculate the current stock price.
ussing the gordon growth model formula the answer will be:
p = D1 / (r - g)
p = $2 / (0.06 - 0.4)
p = $100
The shares are $60 overvalued in the market according to the gordon growth model.
Why use gordon growth model?
Gordon growth model is used to determine the relationship between growth rates, discount rates and valuation. The model demonstrates a clear relation between valuation and return.
